Katalin Karikó

"If there was no pandemic, now no one knows who I am".

/Karikó Katalin/

Lifeway:

She was born in Szolnok, Hungary in 1955. She wanted to be a scientist since the age of 16. She has been researching mRNA continuously for 30 years. Her main field of research is cancer research. She is one of the world's best-known professors. A biochemical researcher developing a vaccine against Covid-19 who patented mRNA technology with her collegaue medical scientist Dreww Weissman already in 2005. The unique discovery will go beyond pandemic and represents a new approach to overcoming future epidemics. Her scientific achievements made it possible to face the Covid19 pandemic that erupted in the spring of 2020. With technology that can be shared quickly with the World Wide Web and technology with research associates professors of virology and immunology, Kizzmekia Corbet and Barney Graham , pharmaceutical companies have been able to quickly develop vaccines, saving many millions of lives.

After graduating from the University of Szeged, she worked at the Biological Research Center of Szeged from 1978-82.

In 1985 she moved with her family, husband, and daughter to the United States, where she first continued her research at Temple University in Philadelphia, then in Washington and at the University of Pennsylvania until 2013.

From 2013 she joined the German pharmaceutical company BioNtech GmbH, of which she became vice president.
